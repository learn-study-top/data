 2. State whether the following statements are true or false. Give reasons for your answer: It is necessary to end terrorism to ensure human security.

The above statement is True.

Reasons:

  1. The biggest challenge to human security is that of terrorism.
  2. It is the biggest challenge to India's internal security and now it is a global problem.
  3. Terrorism targets common, innocent people.
  4. It aims at creating terror or fear in the minds of the people, so that they begin to feel insecure. Therefore, it is necessary to put an end to terrorism to ensure human security.
 Every nation creates a strong security system for itself.

The above statement is True.

Reasons:

  1. There is always a possibility of conflict of interest among the nations.
  2. A nation attacks another and challenges its sovereignty and endangers its security.
  3. In such a situation, it is the duty of a nation to defend itself. For this purpose, every nation creates a strong security system for itself.
 There are no issues of dispute between India and Pakistan.

The above statement is False.

Reasons:

  1. Pakistan has tried to threaten India's national security since independence.
  2. India and Pakistan have a dispute over the Kashmir issue, river water sharing, the problem of infiltration and dispute over the border.
  3. India and Pakistan have gone to war in 1947, 1965, 1971, and 1998.
 3. Explain the following concepts: The functions of the Rapid Action Force

  1. The Rapid Action Force is a paramilitary force.
  2. It performs the task of bringing people's lives to normalcy by moving in quickly in incidents of threat to national security such as bomb blasts or riots.
 Human Security

  1. The term national security has become broader in the post-Cold War period and now includes the security of the people living within the country.
  2. Human security refers to human-centric thinking. It is expected that human beings should be protected from dangers and should be given opportunities for education, health, and development.
  3. It also includes the idea that a conducive environment should be created for everybody to live a respectable life by overcoming illiteracy, poverty, superstition, and backwardness.
  4. Human security necessitates the protection of the rights of minorities and weaker sections.
 Home Guard

  1. The organization Home Guards was established in the pre-independence period to enable the citizens to assist the defense forces.
  2. Any citizen in the age group of 20 to 35 years can join the Home Guards.
  3. The Home Guards maintain public security.
  4. They supply milk, water, and other essential services during times of riots or strikes.
  5. They regulate traffic and help people in times of natural disasters.

Project Arrange a Seminar in your school on ‘the Challenges in front of India’s Security’.

Challenges in front of India’s Security are as follows:

  1. India’s two neighbors Pakistan and China have been threatening India’s security since Independence. The border dispute with China over Arunachal is very long. Similarly, there are several issues with Pakistan like the Kashmir issue, Indus water issue, infiltrations, etc.
  2. India’s security is also challenged internally. Several rebellious movements based on ideology, race-ethnicity, and economic inequality are creating instability. The issue of Naxalism is the biggest threat among them.
  3. Terrorism has threatened the entire world and consequently India too. The events like the Mumbai attack represent similar threats.
