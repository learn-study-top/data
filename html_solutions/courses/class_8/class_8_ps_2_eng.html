 Answer the following questions in brief: On what principles is India's foreign policy based?

India's foreign policy is based on principles like promoting international peace and security, maintaining just relations between nations, and encouraging dispute resolution through arbitration.

 Who has contributed to improving Sino-Indian relations?

The efforts of leaders like Pandit Nehru, Rajiv Gandhi, and Atal Bihari Vajpayee played significant roles in improving Sino-Indian relations, including the signing of the 'Panchsheel' agreement in 1955.

 Write the objectives of India's foreign policy.

Besides the objectives spelt out in the Directive Principles of State Policy, the other objectives of India's foreign policy are as follows:

  1. To ensure that the defence and security of India is not affected while maintaining friendly relations with neighbouring countries.
  2. Not to compromise about the geographical boundaries of the nation.
  3. To protect the unity and integrity of the country.
  4. To protect the interests of India's citizens living in other countries through Indian embassies there.
  5. To establish economic and commercial relations with other countries for achieving economic development of India.

India had trade relations with the following countries in ancient times:

  1. India had trade relations with China, Ceylon (Sri Lanka), and Egypt during the Indus Valley Civilization. Silver, animals, and precious stones were traded. Studies suggest there was significant trade between Harappa and Mesopotamia, mainly through barter.
  2. During the later Vedic period, foreign trade continued with countries like Babylon.
  3. Alexander’s invasion helped establish trade between India and West Asia.
  4. In various periods, such as the Mauryan, Gupta, and Sangam ages, both internal and external trade were well-organized and a significant source of resources.
