 Explain the following concepts: Cold War
  1. The concept of Cold War is used to describe the condition where there is no actual war, but there are such tensions in the circumstances, that they may be responsible to cause war.
  2. There is no open war between the two countries, but there was such tension in their relations that it seemed that a war would erupt at any time.
 Non-alignment
  1. In the era of the cold war, while the world was becoming bipolar, there were some nations like India, which did not want to join the super power rivalry.
  2. Such nations decided to stay out of the cold war rivalry. Their policy is known as Non-alignment.
 Interdependence
  1. Interdependence means that all countries in the world are dependent on each other.
  2. Even a developed nation like America is not self-sufficient in all its needs.
  3. So, however big, small or prosperous a nation is, it is dependent on the other nations.
  4. It is an important feature of the contemporary international system.
 Bipolarisation
  1. During the era of Cold War, America and the Soviet Union emerged as super powers and formed their military alliances.
  2. Most countries of the world joined either of the two super power blocs.
  3. Such a division of the nations of the world into two groups is described as Bipolarisation.
 Globalisation
  1. In 1990's, after the end of the Cold War, trade and economic relations between countries became more open.
  2. There was free flow of capital, technology, labour, markets and information across the world.
  3. The boundaries between nations did not remain as sacrosanct as they were before.
  4. There was give and take of ideas among the people all over the world. All these processes are together called Globalisation.

What were the factors responsible for the end of Cold War?

The factors responsible for the end of the Cold War are as follows:

  1. The Soviet Union adopted the policy of opening up the economy and loosening the state control on the economy.
  2. The then president of the Soviet Union, Mikhail Gorbachev implemented the policies of Perestroika (Restructuring) and Glasnost (Openness). The Soviet economy and polity were restructured.
  3. He reduced control over the press. This gave an impetus to democracy in Russia.
  4. The East European countries challenged 'socialist' governments. These countries broke free from the influence of the Soviet Union and adopted a democratic capitalist path.
  5. Disintegration of the Soviet Union contributed to the end of the Cold War.
 What major changes occured in global politics after the end of the Cold War?

Major changes after the end of the Cold War:

  1. Global politics became unipolar, with America remaining the only superpower.
  2. An atmosphere conducive to the growth in trade and economic relations between and among nations emerged.
  3. Capital, labour, market, technology, ideas and information freely circulated throughout the world.
  4. The idea of giving 'aid' to other nations fell behind, as all nations of the world decided to give priority to trade relations.
  5. The description of a country which was opposed to another changed from 'enemy nation' to 'rival nation.'
  6. The responsibility of the United Nations has increased. It is now required to take concrete steps to maintain global peace and security.
